2. Storage Solutions — Where Your Repaired Photos Should Live

A. Cloud Storage (Easiest to use)

Recommended for small → medium libraries.

  • Google One
  • iCloud+
  • Microsoft OneDrive
  • Amazon Photos

Strengths: auto-sync, easy sharing, built-in AI search.
Weakness: expensive at high TB sizes.

B. External Drives (Best price per TB)

Perfect for home users with 20–200k photos.

  • Samsung T7/T9 SSDs
  • WD My Passport Ultra
  • Seagate Expansion Desktop (8–16 TB)

Strengths: fast, affordable, easy.
Weakness: single point of failure → must be backed up.

C. NAS Systems (For huge libraries)

For users with 100k+, or anyone preserving family history long-term.

  • Synology DS220+ / DS923+
  • QNAP TS-230 / TS-462

Strengths: RAID protection, centralised library, remote access.
Weakness: higher upfront cost.

3. Backup Strategy — The Premium Optimal Rule (3–2–1)

Your restored library should never be in only one place. This is the industry gold standard for preserving irreplaceable memories.

The 3-2-1 Rule

  • 3 copies of your data
  • 2 different types of storage (e.g., SSD + Cloud)
  • 1 off-site copy (Cloud or NAS at a relative's house)

Recommended Combo Examples

  • NAS + external SSD + cloud
  • 2× external drives + cloud
  • Cloud + NAS + Google Takeout export
